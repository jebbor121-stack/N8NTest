Food Safety Tips

General Guidelines

  • Wash your hands: Always wash your hands with soap and water before handling food.
  • Clean surfaces: Keep kitchen surfaces and utensils clean to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Separate raw and cooked foods: Use different cutting boards and utensils for raw meat, poultry, seafood, and vegetables.
  • Cook food to the right temperature: Use a food thermometer to ensure food is cooked to a safe internal temperature.
  • Chill food promptly: Refrigerate perishable foods within two hours (or one hour if the temperature is above 90°F).

Specific Tips

  • Meat and Poultry: Cook to the recommended internal temperatures. Ground meat should reach 160°F, poultry 165°F.
  • Seafood: Cook fish until it flakes easily with a fork.
  • Eggs: Cook eggs until the yolks and whites are firm.
  • Fruits and Vegetables: Wash all fruits and vegetables thoroughly under running water.
  • Leftovers: Store leftovers in shallow containers to cool quickly and consume within 3-4 days.

Preventing Cross-Contamination

  • Use separate cutting boards: Designate separate cutting boards for raw meats and vegetables.
  • Wash utensils: Wash all utensils and cutting boards with hot, soapy water after each use.
  • Avoid contact: Prevent raw meat from touching other foods.
